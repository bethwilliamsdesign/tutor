About Me

I'm a Chartered Civil Engineer and practicing structural engineer working on building projects across the UK. My job requires using maths and physics on a daily basis, and to visualise problems from both these disciplines in a tangible way.

Alongside my practice work, I have been teaching and tutoring engineering at local universities and to other construction professionals for over 10 years. I have taught in lecture environments, small grounps and one-on-one settings to both engineering students, and non-engineers trying to get a better understanding of the maths and physics principles which underpin my work.

Following on from my A-levels, I read Structural Engineering and Architecture at The University of Sheffield. I was keen to balance my love of mathematics and physics with the creative and practical challenge of architecture. Being in Sheffield also lead me to my love of climbing - which I like to think of as 'structural engineering on the go'! I find I am drawn to hobbies and activities that are maths and physics problems in everyday life.

As a neurodivergent person, I am constantly challenging the status quo and findng new ways of approaching problems. This approach helps me to be flexible in my approach, and allows me to tailor my sessions to the individual student's needs and their own unique way of learning.
